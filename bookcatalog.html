Book Title: The Palace of Illusion

Author:Chitra Banerjee Divakaruni

Genre:Mythology

Number of Copies: 55

Available Copies: 20

Summary:"The Palace of Illusions"by Chitra Banerjee Divakaruni is a retelling of the Indian epic, the Mahabharata, from the perspective of Draupadi, the central female character. The novel explores her life, emotions, and struggles as she navigates a male-dominated world. It delves into her relationships with her five husbands (the Pandavas), her complex connection with Lord Krishna, and her secret longing for Karna, her husbands' enemy. The story humanizes Draupadi, giving voice to her desires, pain, and quest for identity in a time of war and politics.


Book Title:The subtle art of not giving a F*ck

Author:Mark Manson

Genre:Self-Help

Number of Copies: 21

Available Copies: 8

Summary:"The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck" by Mark Manson is a self-help book that encourages readers to focus on what truly matters by letting go of the pressure to be positive all the time. Manson argues that life is limited, and we should be selective about where we invest our time and energy, choosing values and goals that align with our personal growth. Through blunt humor and real-life examples, the book emphasizes accepting hardships, embracing failure, and taking responsibility for our choices as a path to a more fulfilling life.


Book Title:The Mile High

Author:Tom Lizford

Genre:Adult Contemporary Romance

Number of Copies: 33

Available Copies: 8

Summary: "The Mile High" by Liz Tomforde is a sports romance novel centered around the relationship between Zander Hawke, a notorious hockey player, and Stevie Lancaster, a flight attendant. Zander is known for his reckless behavior, while Stevie is strong-willed and independent. When they meet on the team's private jet, sparks fly, but their relationship faces challenges due to Zander's fame and commitment issues. The story explores themes of love, personal growth, and overcoming insecurities, all set against the glamorous yet turbulent world of professional sports.

Book Title:The Haunting of Hill House

Author:Shirley Jackson

Genre: Horror

Number of Copies: 15

Available Copies: 6

Summary: "The Haunting of Hill House" by Shirley Jackson is a psychological horror novel about a group of strangers invited to investigate paranormal activity in an old, eerie mansion called Hill House. The main character, Eleanor Vance, a lonely and vulnerable woman, becomes increasingly affected by the house's sinister presence. As strange occurrences escalate, the line between reality and the supernatural blurs. The novel builds an atmosphere of creeping dread, exploring themes of fear, madness, and the power of haunted spaces. It leaves readers questioning whether the horrors are real or a manifestation of Eleanor's mind.

Book Title:The Panchatantra

Author: Vishnu Sharma

Genre: Fables and Moral Tales

Number of Copies: 15

Available Copies: 6

Summary:The Panchatantra is an ancient Indian collection of fables written by Vishnu Sharma. It consists of five books, each containing stories primarily featuring animals that symbolize human traits. The tales are designed to teach wisdom and practical life lessons, focusing on themes such as friendship, leadership, loyalty, and conflict resolution. The stories are framed around a central narrative where a king asks a wise man, Vishnu Sharma, to teach his sons the art of ruling. Through the fables, the sons learn about cleverness, diplomacy, and how to navigate complex social and political situations. Each story concludes with a moral, making the Panchatantra both entertaining and educational.
